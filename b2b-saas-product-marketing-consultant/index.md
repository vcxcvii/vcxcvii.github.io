If three people at your company describe the product three different ways, that is not a messaging problem you can fix with a document. It shows up as slow deals, discounting, sales decks nobody uses, and launches that land with a thud.

That is usually a positioning problem, and it is fixable.

## What does a B2B SaaS product marketing consultant do?

A product marketing consultant helps decide who the product is for, why buyers should care, how the company should tell the story, and what sales, marketing, and product need to take it to market.

The output is not one messaging document. It is a clearer product story that shows up in the website, launch, campaign, sales call, product, and pipeline.

## What you can hire me to do

### Position the product

Choose the ICP, problem, category, value, proof, and message order. Turn internal product knowledge into a story buyers understand and sales can repeat.

### Launch a product or feature

Set the goal, audience, story, plan, sales readiness, campaign, and measures. Treat a launch as a growth bet, not a list of tasks with a date.

### Help sales win

Build pitch stories, use-case plays, battlecards, objection answers, deal support, and content reps can find while the deal is live.

### Improve PLG and lifecycle

Connect product use to activation, adoption, expansion, and the right sales conversation. Build campaigns and in-product messages around what users do, not only what marketing wants to announce.

### Build the product marketing system

Set up research, requests, priorities, shared documents, AI workflows, launch reviews, and ownership so the work stays useful after the first project.

## The request is often not the real problem

The homepage rewrite may be an ICP decision nobody wants to make.

The launch plan may be weak because every feature is treated as important.

Sales may ignore the battlecard because it does not help with a live deal.

The new message may fail because the product, website, campaign, and sales story changed at different times.

A messaging framework nobody uses is not product marketing. It is a PDF.

## What we measure

Product marketing should have numbers, but the right number depends on the work.

- Positioning and website work: message testing, conversion, qualified pipeline, and sales feedback.
- Launches: reach, interest, adoption, pipeline, revenue, and what the market taught us.
- Sales enablement: use by reps, deal conversion, sales cycle, objections, and win or loss reasons.
- PLG and lifecycle: activation, adoption, expansion, retention, and revenue.

We agree on the result before the work starts. "People liked the new deck" is not enough.

## How the work happens

**Find the truth.** Talk to customers, sales, product, founders, and the people closest to the number. Review calls, data, lost deals, current messages, and product use.

**Make the choice.** Choose the audience, problem, value, proof, message, goal, and work that matters now.

**Ship the whole story.** Connect the strategy to the page, launch, campaign, sales material, product message, and review.

**Watch the result.** Look at use, conversion, pipeline, adoption, and feedback. Change the story or plan when the evidence says it is wrong.

AI helps with research, call analysis, first drafts, testing, sales support, and keeping shared context current. It does not invent customer proof or make the final decision.
