## What does a B2B SaaS product marketing consultant do?

A B2B SaaS product marketing consultant helps a company decide who the product is for, why buyers should care, how the story should be told, and what sales and marketing need to take that story to market.

That is the clean definition.

The messy version is that product marketing gets called when sales says the deck is wrong, product says the launch is late, the founder rewrites the homepage, and nobody can explain why the last campaign failed.

## Most positioning problems are operating problems wearing a copy hat

A new tagline will not fix five teams making five different decisions about the customer.

A messaging framework will not help if it gets approved, exported to PDF, and forgotten.

A launch tier will not create focus if every feature is treated as urgent.

Good product marketing is not a document factory. It is the system that keeps product truth, buyer truth, sales reality, and company strategy from drifting apart.

## Problems I work on

### Positioning and messaging

ICP, category, differentiated value, proof, message hierarchy, homepage narrative, and the uncomfortable choices needed to make them coherent.

### Product and feature launches

Launch strategy, audience, story, internal readiness, campaign structure, sales enablement, and measurement. Not a checklist where every box is green and nobody remembers the launch.

### Sales enablement

Competitive narratives, pitch stories, battlecards, objection handling, use-case plays, and content reps can find when a deal is live.

### PLG and lifecycle

Activation, expansion, in-product education, lifecycle campaigns, and the bridge between product behavior and revenue conversations.

### Product marketing operating systems

Intake, prioritization, research, repositories, AI workflows, review cadence, and ownership. The machinery behind the artifacts.

## How I work

First, I talk to the people closest to the truth: customers, sales, product, founders, and whoever owns the number.

Then I reduce the problem. One audience. One decision. One motion. One definition of finished.

Then we ship the connected pieces. Strategy and execution stay together long enough to learn something.

I will use AI aggressively for research, analysis, drafting, synthesis, and QA. I will not use it to manufacture customer evidence or pretend a first draft is final.

## What I will tell you honestly

- Your ICP may be broad because leadership does not want to choose.
- Your "category creation" idea may be ordinary positioning with a bigger budget.
- Sales may ignore enablement because the asset is bad, not because adoption needs a campaign.
- Your homepage may be accurate and still say nothing.
- The launch may not deserve a launch.
- More content may make the problem worse.

Product marketing without judgment becomes project management with better slides.

## When should you hire a consultant?

Hire one when the problem crosses teams, internal capacity is thin, and senior judgment is needed before you add permanent headcount.

Do not hire one because saying "we brought in an expert" feels like progress.

If the product is pre-PMF, leadership will not make tradeoffs, or nobody can own implementation internally, fix that first.

## Consultant versus fractional product marketing lead

A consultant solves a bounded problem and helps your team build the answer.

A fractional lead owns an ongoing function, cadence, stakeholder relationships, and decision quality over time.
